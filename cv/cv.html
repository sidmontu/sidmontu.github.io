Personal Profile

I am interested in novel computing architectures that aim to meet computing demands of the future. My doctoral research revolves around developing efficient Network-on-Chip (NoC) overlays that serve as power efficient hardware accelerators for notoriously difficult-to-parallelize problems. My current focus is on leveraging the dataflow computing paradigm to accelerate graph problems. I am currently looking for career opportunities (both industry or postdoctoral positions) that allow me to apply and improve my circuit design skillsets. I am also highly interested in developing efficient chips for accelerating specific application domains such as molecular dynamics, bioinformatics, CNNs/DNNs, and continuous speech recognition.

I am currently in my 5th and final year of my PhD candidacy at Nanyang Technological University (NTU) of Singapore. Research Assistant

July 2012 - September 2012

Circuits & Systems Research Group, EEE, Imperial College London

This was a 3-month research attachment under a University Research Opportunities Programme (UROP) at Imperial College London. My work involved improving a Java-based software backend for optimizing dataflow graphs for improved runtime performance on custom Network-on-Chip (NoC) architectures. I also wrote extensions for a cycle-accurate simulator for the newer NoC designs that enabled us to explore a large design space efficiently. The engineering work in this project eventually contributed to several conference publications in FCCM 2014, FPL 2014, and FPT 2014.
