Personal Profile

I am interested in novel computing architectures that aim to meet computing demands of the future. My doctoral research revolves around developing efficient Network-on-Chip (NoC) overlays that serve as power efficient hardware accelerators for notoriously difficult-to-parallelize problems. My current focus is on leveraging the dataflow computing paradigm to accelerate graph problems. I am currently looking for career opportunities (both industry or postdoctoral positions) that allow me to apply and improve my circuit design skillsets. I am also highly interested in developing efficient chips for accelerating specific application domains such as continuous speech recognition, computer vision, and artificial intelligence.
